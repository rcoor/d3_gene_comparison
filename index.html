We are all unqiue in some way. Facial features, emotions, way of thinking, etc. are all features that seperately or together define us as being unique. These differences often seem significant enough for us to be able to classify and recognize other people. In truth, many of the things that makes us unique are woven into our DNA as single mutations that often makes the whole difference.

A lot of data to compare with

Your genetic fingerprint; So what do your genes tell about you, and how are you unique from everyone else? Mutations are really just differences in DNA that varies from most people, thus classification of mutations into phenotypical alterations is a natural way of visualising gene data.



288 people have been probed for 240.000 mutations where approximately 100 SNPs have been linked to categories within fitness, nutrition and lifestyle. Let's have a look of how these people compare to you.

Data

The data was produced from a library of 288 people with 240.000 mutations each. The data was filtered and anonymized before being used in this visualization.

Scoring algorithm

The scoring for each category is based on a weight-scoring algorithm. Litterature on genotypes that are linked to specific categories are reviewed and scores for different genotype variants are applied. Some categories are build from multiple genotypes and some are binary, meaning that the genotype works as a on/off switch for a specific phenotype. The sum of contribution for each genotype is used as a max score for the given category. When a test is being done on a person the genotypes are linked to the scores and a score is applied. This is then compared with the max score and thus a percentage score can be calculated. The outcome of a category is then set as bounds in percentage, e.g. score <=2 5 gives low, score <=5 0 gives medium low, score <=7 5 medium high, etc. Each category is also applied a confidence score (not shown here).

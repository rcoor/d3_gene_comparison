Who are you? Genetically speaking

By Theis Hjalte Thorn Jakobsen and Christian Gr√ºner JAN 18, 2017

We are all unqiue in some way. Facial features, emotions, way of thinking, etc. are all features that seperately or together define us as being unique. These differences often seem significant enough for us to be able to classify and recognize other people. In truth, many of the things that makes us unique are woven into our DNA as single mutations that often makes the whole difference.

Your genetic thumbprint

A weight-scoring algorithm has taken mutational data-points and given scores to different categories. The algorithm is based on reviewed scientific litterature and peoples genoptypes (mutations).

By showing your data on a radar chart you essentially get your unique profile as a thumbprint, which is unique to you. Here it has been compared to an average person made from the accumulated data of 217 people. The data can be filtered into 4 areas, all, nutrition, fitness, and lifestyle.

Hover on a dot to see the outcome of the result.

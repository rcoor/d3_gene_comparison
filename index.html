We are all unqiue in some way. Facial features, emotions, way of thinking, etc. are all features that seperately or together define us as being unique. These differences often seem significant enough for us to be able to classify and recognize other people. In truth, many of the things that makes us unique are woven into our DNA as single mutations that often makes the whole difference.

A lot of data to compare with

Your genetic fingerprint; So what do your genes tell about you, and how are you unique from everyone else? Mutations are really just differences in DNA that varies from most people, thus classification of mutations into phenotypical alterations is a natural way of visualising gene data.



288 people have been probed for 240.000 mutations where approximately 100 SNPs have been linked to categories within fitness, nutrition and lifestyle. Let's have a look of how these people compare to you.

Your genetic fingerprint

By showing your data on a radar chart you essentially get your unique profile as a fingerprint, which is unique to you. Here it has been compared to an average person made from the accumulated data of 288 people. The data can be filtered into 4 areas, all, nutrition, fitness, and lifestyle.

The radar chart

Each category have been given a percentage score based their phenotypical penetrance. Hover over a point to see it's outcome.
